I'm a GIS Engineer at GAIT Global, with a passion for unraveling the mysteries of aquatic ecosystems and contributing to the global understanding of blue carbon dynamics with a keen interest in conservation and ecosystem restoration. As a GIS Engineer specializing in aquatic and blue carbon research, I have been at the forefront of developing innovative spatial models and tools to map and monitor these critical ecosystems. My work involves integrating satellite imagery, field data, and advanced GIS techniques to assess the extent and health of coastal habitats. By employing cutting-edge technologies, I contribute valuable insights into the mitigation of climate change through the preservation and restoration of blue carbon ecosystems.

I recently completed Master of Science in Geoinformatics from Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra, in Ranchi, India, where I developed a strong foundation in mapping, spatial analysis, and remote sensing and my Bachelor's degree in Chemistry from the Rama Devi Women's University, in Bhubaneswar, India. I believe in empowering local communities with the knowledge to sustainably manage and protect the coastal resources.
